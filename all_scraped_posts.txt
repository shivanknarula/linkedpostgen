ROS2 learning project 🪛 
Recently I read the book - "ROS 2 from scratch" by Edouard Renard . I dived more into the whole robotics simple nodes and one of the concepts there was SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping).
Through this experience , I worked with : 
1️⃣ ROS 2 workplace structure and python nodes . 
2️⃣ colcon build system and package management . 
3️⃣ ROS 2 executable and workflows( ros2_run) .
4️⃣ Basic knowledge and understanding of robotics. 
5️⃣ Linux ( Ubuntu 24.04) env . 
🧠 Key Takeaways
One of the biggest insights from this project is that:
the core algorithms in robotics are not the hardest part 
— the environment is. 
I find myself really fighting against the terminal when I tried to install humble first for the ros2 , later on when I read the book and the docs online I understood "humble" isn't suitable for Ubuntu 24.04 , but "Jazzy" is the right pkg. overall , I came myself to conclusion that I had to remove everything and start over twice . (Because: The version of ubuntu isn't matched and it caused broken pkgs , and the whole env didn't run at all) 
🚀 What I’m building toward
I am currently progressing toward:
SLAM mapping systems
Autonomous navigation
Robot perception using sensors (LiDAR, camera)
Real-world robotics simulation pipelines
💡 Final Reflection
This project taught me that in robotics:
“The algorithm is often the easy part. The real challenge is making everything work together in a real system.”
